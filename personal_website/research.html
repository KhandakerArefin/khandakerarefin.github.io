Research & Projects

Here you can explore the research projects and hardware designs I have been working on, ranging from neural network accelerators to embedded robotics. Each project combines rigorous engineering with a passion for pushing the limits of hardware efficiency and capability.

Edge AI Accelerator

This on‑going project develops an application‑specific integrated circuit (ASIC) for accelerating artificial neural networks (ANNs) on microcontrollers via SPI. The accelerator contains parallel multiply–accumulate units and on‑chip memory, allowing microcontrollers to execute inference with minimal power overhead. It has been tested on MNIST, Fashion MNIST, CIFAR‑10 and CIFAR‑100 across various architectures.

Supervisor: Prof. Dr. Satyendra N. Biswas – Tools: ModelSim, MATLAB, Cadence Virtuoso

Efficient Floating‑Point Unit

Traditional floating‑point units consume excessive cycles and power in neural network accelerators. I designed a logarithmic multiplication and addition unit that dramatically reduces area and energy with only minimal accuracy loss. When tested on datasets such as MNIST and CIFAR‑10, the accelerator achieved accuracy within 1 % of conventional MATLAB implementations.

GlobalFoundries 12nm & 22nm – Cadence Virtuoso, ModelSim

Human Detection Robot

For my undergraduate thesis I created a personal assistant robot with voice control and navigation using a Raspberry Pi. The robot communicates over Wi‑Fi using gesture, voice and gyroscope inputs and demonstrates applications in home and hospital environments.

Supervisor: Kazi Tauseef Mohammad – TensorFlow, Raspberry Pi

Motor Driver & Charger Unit

Designed a high‑performance motor driver and charging circuitry for the Red‑X team competing in the efficient vehicle challenge at Technofest 2022. This design enabled an electric vehicle to operate efficiently under competition constraints.

Supervisor: Kazi Tauseef Mohammad – Proteus

Avionic System Design

Developed the avionic system for a model rocket as part of the AUST Rocketry Challenge at Technofest 2022. The system integrated sensors and control logic to ensure stable flight and telemetry.

Supervisor: Prof. Dr. Omar Farrok – Proteus, Arduino IDE
